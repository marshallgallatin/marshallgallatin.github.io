## Tip of the Week ##

I'm going to take a break from tips this week and instead discuss current events that are relevant to Computer Science.  Namely, the court order which would force Apple to create software which would allow the US government to unlock the San Bernardino terrorist's iPhone.

* [Reuters](http://www.reuters.com/article/us-apple-encryption-doj-idUSKCN0VS2FT)
* [Tim Cook's response](http://www.apple.com/customer-letter/)
* [Best technical explanation I've found](http://blog.trailofbits.com/2016/02/17/apple-can-comply-with-the-fbi-court-order/)

I have discussed this with some of my co-workers and we're all in complete agreement that having Apple implement a backdoor to unlock the iPhone is opening a pandora's box.  Once this backdoor exists, hackers and governments around the world will begin looking for it, and chances are someone will find it and exploit it.  I am not an Apple fanboy, and I don't oppose the US government's attempt to unlock the iPhone, especially since the user of the phone is deceased, but this feels like a slipery slope.  Would you buy a safe that had a master combination?  What if a regime known to persecute its own people for their beliefs requested the same access?  Since the software doesn't exist, who pays for its development?
