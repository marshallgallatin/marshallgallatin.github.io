### What will you do next week? ###

Hopefully I'll be starting project 2, and catching up on the reading.  Luckily, the quiz on Friday had nothing to do with the readings on Thursday.  Unluckily, the quiz was over some fun tuples vs lists topics, which I completely didn't understand with prior to the quiz.  Oh well, experience is the best teacher, [the more you know](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GD6qtc2_AQA).

I find that one of the hardest things for me to adjust to, switching from C++ to Python, is the type and variable system.  In C++ its VERY clear about what is a reference or an address or an actual instance.  Python just seems to _take care of it all_ for you, and by take care of it all, I mean it just magically makes copies of things for you when necessary.  I'm not sure I like this, but I'm keeping an open mind.

The other thing that amazes me about Python is it's succinctness.  I came up with a similar solution to my OOP project, and yet the Python version was 100 lines shorter.  Shorter, but my C++ implementation would blow the Python version out of the water when it comes to performance.  I read on piazza that someone said their implementation ranked 17 on Sphere.  I must be missing something when it comes to Python, 17 on Sphere has a time of something like 0.01.  I thought my implementation was pretty good: I had two caches, both preloaded, never calculating any collatz cycle more than once, all the in class optimizations.  My version was accepted by Sphere, and even with all these optimizations, my time was only 1.34.  Really? More than 100 times worse?  I wish I knew what they did.

I'm starting to think that learning Python is easy, but being good at Python is hard.
