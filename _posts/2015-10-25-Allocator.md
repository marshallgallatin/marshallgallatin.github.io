Another week down, another project done.  I thought the allocator project was interesting, and subtlety difficult.  Not that I thought it was super hard, it was just difficult to nail down the exact behavior required from the specifications.  I'm really not completely pleased with my submission, I think I let the project creep up on me a little bit.  I feel like if I had another day or two I could have had a really good solution, but I think I just had an okay one.

I wonder how professors determine the time at which assignments are due.  For this class, our projects are due at 10pm, which I find a tad early in the evening.  But this is by no means standard, some classes have an 11:59pm deadline, some classes are due just before the class time.  Oh well, I learn to live with whats required.  Alas, this is pretty off topic, back to OOP....

Class this week was alright.  Quizzes are back.  We learned about how vectors were implemented.  I always wonder how close the in class implementations are to the real thing.  In my head, the real implementations are some crazy, cryptic, ununderstandable optimized beast of code.  We also discussed the explicit and friend keywords.  Other than functions needing access to classes in order to do things like equality, I don't know what another good use for friend is.  I feel like if you need to use friend between two classes, then chances are one or both of those classes is designed poorly.  I'd like to see an example for something other than an operator to understand what other uses it has.

Our next project was assigned today.  Looks interesting, something called Darwin's world.  It looks like a step up in difficulty from the previous project, so I think I'm going to try and get started earlier than the last project.  It looks like it has quite a bit of output, which worries me a bit - thats one of those things that is easy to make a mistake thats hard to track down.
