Well, this blogpost is late.  I apologize to my readers for the tardiness of my post.  This week has been difficult to say the least, none of it has anything to do with OOP.  I'm currently taking Algorithms and Complexity (CS 331), and the second test took priority over OOP for the week.  Alas, I digress.  This blog is about OOP, so I will spare you the details of Dynamic Programming.  

This week in OOP we were given an overview of the project thats due on Thursday.  Wow, its due in 3 days, and honestly I've barely started.  I'm worried this time, the Darwin project is subtlety difficult.  On the bright side, we are given pretty much free reign to implement it as we see fit.  On the downside, I have to figure out UML to make a diagram.  Ugh, I hate documentation (I think this is a common sentiment among programmers).

We had a guest speaker this week, Conrad Stoll from Mutual Mobile.  I swear, every time we have a speaker, I get a little ticked off that I'm older than them.  Conrad did a lot of talking about open source projects relating to mobile app development.  I have to admit, I'm not sure I'd like being an app developer, and he didn't make me any more enthusiastic about it.  I guess it ranks about 1 notch higher than a database developer.  Personally, I like the idea of open source software, I'm writing this on an open source Linux distribution running an open source text editor.  Pretty sweet.  Maybe after I graduate I'll have time to contribute - I've always been interested in contributing to a project, but until recently, I really haven't been confident enough in my coding ability to try.

## Tip of the week ##

Just because its Open Source Software doesn't necessarily mean you can't get paid to work on it.  Check [this article](http://www.marketwatch.com/story/the-linux-foundation-announces-project-to-advance-real-time-linux-2015-10-05) out about the Real-Time Linux Collaborative Project. Companies like Google, Texas Instruments, IBM, Intel, ARM, and National Instruments are members (which lets be honest - it means they're putting up money to have people work on this project).
